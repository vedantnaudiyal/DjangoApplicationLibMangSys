Title: To Kill a Mockingbird
Genres: Fiction, Southern Gothic, Bildungsroman
Authors: Harper Lee
Author's Date of Birth: April 28, 1926
Author's Date of Death: February 19, 2016
Language: English
ISBN No: 978-0-06-112008-4
Overview:
"To Kill a Mockingbird" is a novel by Harper Lee set in the American South during the 1930s. It explores themes of racial injustice and moral growth through the eyes of Scout Finch, a young girl whose father, lawyer Atticus Finch, defends a black man falsely accused of raping a white woman.

Title: Pride and Prejudice
Genres: Novel, Romance, Comedy of Manners
Authors: Jane Austen
Author's Date of Birth: December 16, 1775
Author's Date of Death: July 18, 1817
Language: English
ISBN No: 978-1-61720-103-9
Overview:
"Pride and Prejudice" by Jane Austen is a novel set in rural England, focusing on the lively Bennet family and their five unmarried daughters. The story primarily revolves around the romance between the witty Elizabeth Bennet and the wealthy, aloof Mr. Darcy, exploring themes of love, marriage, and social class.

Title: One Hundred Years of Solitude
Genres: Magic Realism, Novel, Epic
Authors: Gabriel García Márquez
Author's Date of Birth: March 6, 1927
Author's Date of Death: April 17, 2014
Language: Spanish (original), translated into various languages including English
ISBN No: 978-0-06-088328-7
Overview:
"One Hundred Years of Solitude" is a landmark novel by Gabriel García Márquez, telling the multi-generational story of the Buendía family in the fictional town of Macondo. It blends elements of magical realism with themes of love, war, politics, and the cyclical nature of history.
